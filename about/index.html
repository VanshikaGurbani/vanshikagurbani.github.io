About

From notebook to production

I'm Vanshika, an AI Engineer and Data Scientist with an M.S. in Statistics (Data Science) from Rutgers University and a B.E. in Computer Science from the University of Mumbai. I specialize in building intelligent systems that bridge ML research with production-grade software.

Currently at Rutgers' Particulate Systems Lab, I'm rebuilding legacy MATLAB models into physics-informed ML baselines, achieving ~40% faster inference with <5% prediction error and cutting validation time by 60%. The work powers a digital-twin stack for real-time pharmaceutical manufacturing optimization.

Previously at ContinuumCare.ai, I built a RAG-based health assistant integrating 6+ medical guideline sources with 2-3 evidence-backed citations per response. Before that, I spent two years as a Teaching Assistant at Rutgers, teaching 200+ undergraduates foundational computing and earning 95% positive feedback on coding instruction evaluations.

Philosophy

I believe in bridging scientific computing with modern ML, not replacing legacy systems but making them faster, smarter, and more accessible.

I value rigorous evaluation. My BioNER models hit F1=0.98 on gene recognition and F1=0.99 on disease extraction because I benchmark obsessively across datasets.

I build for real users. Whether it's a Streamlit digital-twin dashboard, an AI health assistant, or a coding lab for 200 students, the work has to ship and has to work.
